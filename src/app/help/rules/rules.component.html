Rules

Blackjack, also known as Pontoon, or simply 21, is a popular and entertaining card game played in homes and casinos around the world. There are certainly many variations in the terms used during play, and the exact rules that are followed. The rules for this version of the game are specified below and may differ from those to which you are accustomed.

  • The game uses a standard 52 card deck consisting of two colours and four suits.
  • Aces may be low and worth one point, or high and worth eleven points. Whether aces are low or high must be specifed prior to game commencement. By default they are low.
  • All face cards (King, Queen, and Jack) are worth ten points.
  • All non-face cards are worth their pip value (the number displayed on the card).
  • The object of the game is to win by getting closer to 21 than the dealer, without going over. If your score exceeds 21 you lose immediately, irrespective of the dealer's score.
  • Once the game has commenced, the player will either request another card from the dealer using the button labelled "Hit", or end their turn using the button labelled "Stick". The player my request as many cards as they wish, but once they have stuck, their turn is over and play will move to the dealer.
